Method

No sacred number

The headline EHI score is a weighted mean of included US metrics. Each metric is linearly normalized between a visible low anchor and high anchor, then reversed when lower values are considered healthier. Scores are clipped to 0–100. The historical EHI line combines annual reconstructed fixed-core points with observed full-basket snapshots; backcast rows are explicitly labeled in data.json and do not pretend that newer metrics existed in earlier years.

GOON is calculated separately. Private domain histories are converted to pressure, blended mostly from absolute popularity with a smaller own-history trend term, then conservatively adjusted for novelty, intensity, extremity and interaction architecture. Domains are aggregated inside categories first; category target shares are then combined into one GOON number. This prevents a niche from gaining more voting power just because more sites from that niche were listed.

Trait weighting describes content/platform architecture, not the moral worth or identity of users. A single unusual fantasy is not treated as evidence of real-world intent or pathology. The index is designed to watch population-level patterns and systems, not label individuals.

This is an interpretive dashboard, not a validated psychological scale. A different theory of healthy eros should change the anchors, weights, exclusions, or even the metric list. That is a feature, not a bug.
