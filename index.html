Method

No sacred number

The headline EHI score is a weighted mean of included US metrics. Each metric is linearly normalized between a visible low anchor and high anchor, then reversed when lower values are considered healthier. Scores are clipped to 0–100. Related metrics are assigned to correlation clusters; when a cluster’s raw weights exceed its visible cap, the effective weights are scaled proportionally. More measurements can improve confidence without letting one underlying phenomenon vote five times. The historical EHI line combines annual reconstructed fixed-core points with observed full-basket snapshots; backcast rows are explicitly labeled in data.json and do not pretend that newer metrics existed in earlier years.

GOON is calculated separately. Private domain histories are converted to pressure, blended mostly from absolute popularity with a smaller own-history trend term, then conservatively adjusted for novelty, intensity, extremity and interaction architecture. Domains are aggregated inside categories first; category target shares are then combined into one GOON number. This prevents a niche from gaining more voting power just because more sites from that niche were listed.

Trait weighting describes content/platform architecture, not the moral worth or identity of users. A single unusual fantasy is not treated as evidence of real-world intent or pathology. The index is designed to watch population-level patterns and systems, not label individuals.

Every metric carries a signal role, domain, correlation cluster, evidence tier, and mapping-confidence label. Outcomes, orientations, impairment signals, exposure proxies, mechanisms, context, and countercurrents are kept distinct so exposure is not silently promoted into pathology and mechanism studies are not mistaken for national prevalence.

The GOONER/FEMOID meter is separate from the EHI outcome score. Only metrics explicitly marked as behavior-attributable participate. Exposure proxies and orientation signals are discounted relative to impairment signals; evidence tier and mapping confidence also modify directional influence. Each archetype then receives its own normalized severity score, so a side with more available sensors does not win merely by owning more denominator. The public left-right split compares those two severities. Partnership, fertility, marriage rate, and embodied socialization can show that the system is sick without automatically assigning fault. The meter reports raw and confidence-adjusted directional coverage and remains explicitly provisional until both sides meet minimum evidence-breadth rules. The labels are deliberately rude shorthand, not claims that either behavior belongs exclusively to one sex.

This is an interpretive dashboard, not a validated psychological scale. A different theory of healthy eros should change the anchors, weights, exclusions, or even the metric list. That is a feature, not a bug.
